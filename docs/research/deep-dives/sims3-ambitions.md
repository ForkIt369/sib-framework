# Deep Dive: The Sims 3 Ambitions - Prima Official Game Guide

> **Source:** The Sims 3 Ambitions Expansion Pack - Prima Official Game Guide
> **Publisher:** Prima Games (2010)
> **Author:** Catherine Browne
> **Platform:** PC
> **Type:** Strategy Guide / Systems Reference (~188 pages)

---

## Core Essence

Ambitions transforms The Sims 3 career system from a passive rabbit-hole mechanic into an **active profession simulation** where players directly control their Sim's work activities in real-time. The expansion introduces five new active professions (Ghost Hunter, Firefighter, Investigator, Architectural Designer, Stylist), modifies the Medical career into a hybrid active/rabbit-hole track, adds the Inventing and Sculpting skills with full crafting pipelines, introduces Twinbrook as a new explorable world, and layers a self-employment system that lets Sims monetize any skill. The core design principle is agency: instead of sending your Sim to work and waiting, you drive the fire truck, interrogate suspects, renovate rooms, style hair, and bust ghosts. Every profession has a visible job-scoring system, client evaluation mechanics, and skill-based advancement that makes work feel like gameplay rather than a progress bar.

## Key Lexicon

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Active Profession** | A career where the player directly controls the Sim during work hours, performing job-specific tasks in the game world rather than disappearing into a rabbit-hole building |
| **Tones** | Behavioral modes selected while at work (Business As Usual, Work Hard, Take It Easy, etc.) that affect performance metrics, skill development, stress, and co-worker relationships |
| **Ghost Hunter** | Active profession that captures ghosts at residential lots using the Banshee Banisher ($900) and Ghost Wrangling Ghostly Tether; Lv1-10 from Skeptic to Paranormal Expert ($1,800/day) |
| **Firefighter** | Active profession responding to emergencies (fires, earthquakes, meteor strikes) via fire truck; Lv1-10 from Smoke Spotter to Fire Chief ($2,400/day) |
| **Investigator** | Active profession solving cases through interviewing, snooping, surveillance, fingerprinting, and evidence gathering; Lv1-10 from Desk Jockey to Dynamic DNS ($2,400/day) |
| **Architectural Designer** | Active profession renovating rooms for clients using modified Build/Buy Mode; scored on object placement, budget management, and client trait satisfaction |
| **Stylist** | Active profession giving makeovers (outfits, hair, makeup) to clients at the salon or around town; scored on client satisfaction |
| **Medical Career** | Hybrid career track that combines rabbit-hole work with active community tasks (free clinics, vaccinations, local emergencies); Lv1-10 from Organ Donor to World Renowned Surgeon ($2,400/day) |
| **Skill-Based Career** | Self-employment system where Sims register at City Hall to earn income from selling skill products (paintings, novels, sculptures, photos, nectar, inventions, fish, produce) |
| **Inventing** | New skill (Lv1-10) using the Scraptronic Workbench; creates widgets, toys, and special devices from scrap; includes detonation risk |
| **Sculpting** | New skill (Lv1-10) using the Pedestrian Sculpting Station; carve statues from Clay, Stone, Metal, and Ice in styles from Abstract to Masterwork |
| **Tattooing** | New social interaction using the Ink-in-Comfort 3000 Digital Tattoo Applicator; apply custom tattoos to other Sims for tips |
| **Laundry** | New household chore system: dirty clothes accumulate, must be washed in machines and dried (dryer or clothesline) for positive Hygiene moodlets |
| **Twinbrook** | The expansion's new world -- a bayou-themed town with swamps, industrial areas, and a distinct Southern American flavor |
| **Scrap** | Raw material for the Inventing skill; collected from the Junkyard, detonated objects, or purchased at the Scraptronic Workbench |
| **Portfolio** | A career-progression album for Architectural Designer and Stylist professions; photos of completed jobs track progress toward 100% completion |
| **Pension** | A daily income earned after retiring from a career; amount varies by career level at retirement |

## The Vibe

Ambitions reads like a job training manual for the most eccentric career counselor imaginable. The guide treats each profession with deadly seriousness, providing flowcharts for ghost capture protocols, fire truck response routes, investigation interrogation trees, and room renovation scoring algorithms. There is an obsessive attention to scoring criteria: the Architectural Designer section lists 7 specific factors affecting client satisfaction, cross-referenced with 15+ client personality traits that modify scoring. The Investigator profession feels like a detective simulation grafted onto The Sims, complete with fingerprint dusting, trash can rummaging, and witness intimidation. The Firefighter section includes fire truck driving mechanics and heroism scoring. The Skill-Based Career tables reveal a parallel economy where creative output (paintings, sculptures, novels) generates income through a 10-level progression with threshold-based bonuses. The Traditional Careers chapter is a comprehensive reference of every base-game career modified with the new Tones system, complete with salary tables, work schedules, pension values, and promotion metrics -- essentially a complete HR database for a fictional city. The guide's underlying philosophy is that work should be interesting: every career has hidden mechanics, social interactions, and progression paths that reward deep engagement over passive grinding.

## New Mechanics Introduced

### 1. Ghost Hunter Profession (Lv1-10)
- **Equipment:** Banshee Banisher ($900 scanner/weakener), Ghost Wrangling Ghostly Tether (capture device)
- **Career progression:** Skeptic ($375/wk) -> Paranormal Expert ($2,400/day)
- **Work cycle:** Receive job at police station -> Travel to haunted lot -> Locate ghosts with Banshee Banisher -> Weaken with Spirit Sucker interaction -> Capture with Ghostly Tether -> Sell or release captured ghosts
- **Ghost types:** Multiple spirits with varying difficulty; more dangerous ghosts at higher career levels
- **Job scoring:** Speed of capture, number of ghosts per job, client satisfaction
- **Lv5 unlock:** Improved Banshee Banisher (faster detection)
- **Lv10 reward:** Genie lamp chance from captured ghosts

### 2. Firefighter Profession (Lv1-10)
- **Equipment:** Fire truck (drive to emergencies), Fire Axe (break down doors)
- **Career progression:** Smoke Spotter ($200/wk) -> Fire Chief ($2,400/day)
- **Emergency types:** House fires, community lot fires, earthquakes (rescue trapped Sims), meteor strikes
- **Work cycle:** Alarm sounds -> Drive fire truck to location -> Extinguish fire / Rescue Sims -> Return to station
- **Heroism scoring:** Speed of response, number of Sims rescued, fire containment
- **Lv5 unlock:** Siren (clears traffic), Fireproof self-moodlet
- **Lv10 unlock:** Fire Chief title, highest salary, Fire Prevention Inspection interaction
- **Tones:** Business As Usual, Work Hard, Take It Easy, Practice Repairing, Chit Chat, Hit the Showers, Hit the Gym (builds Athletic)

### 3. Investigator Profession (Lv1-10)
- **Career progression:** Desk Jockey ($220/wk) -> Dynamic DNS ($2,400/day)
- **Case types:** Missing persons, theft, cheating spouses, corporate espionage, surveillance
- **Investigation tools:**
  - Interview Sim (friendly/intimidating)
  - Rummage Through Trash (find evidence, Disgusted moodlet)
  - Spy On interaction (surveillance)
  - Dust for Prints (fingerprint evidence)
  - Search Location (find hidden clues)
  - Computer Research (background checks)
- **Case resolution:** Gather sufficient evidence -> Report to client -> Receive payment + career XP
- **Lv3 unlock:** Stake Out interaction
- **Lv5 unlock:** Access to crime computer at police station
- **Lv8 unlock:** Forensic Analysis computer interaction
- **Lv10 reward:** Private Eye office, highest pay
- **Unique socials:** Question, Interrogate, Gather Clues

### 4. Architectural Designer Profession (Lv1-10)
- **Career progression:** Fabric Fetcher ($272/wk) -> Dictator of Design ($2,480/wk)
- **Work cycle:** Receive job (client specifies requirements) -> Enter modified Build/Buy Mode at client's home -> Place required objects within budget -> Complete Renovation -> Client evaluates
- **Scoring factors (7):**
  1. All required objects added
  2. Spoke to client beforehand
  3. Room finishes complete (floor/wall)
  4. Light source in room
  5. Sim can access all objects
  6. Budget management (on target)
  7. Get Measurements interaction (Lv5, bigger tip)
- **Client trait scoring modifiers (15+):**
  - Snobs: penalize for not spending full budget
  - Frugal: bonus for under budget
  - Loves Outdoors: lots of windows bonus
  - Artistic: love decorations/paintings
  - Bookworms: love bookshelves
  - Computer Whizzes: love computers / Technophobes hate electronics
  - Couch Potatoes: bonus for comfort chairs
  - Easily Impressed: like everything
- **Renovation types (25+):** Baby Room, Bedroom, Kitchen, Library, Gym, Entertainment Room, Bachelor Lounge, Sculpting Room, Writing Room, Landscaping, and more
- **Portfolio system:** Photo of each completed job; 100% completion goal
- **Research Design:** Drafting table sketches (develops Painting skill, adds to portfolio)

### 5. Stylist Profession (Lv1-10)
- **Career progression:** Fashion Fledgling ($182/wk) -> Fashion Phenomenon ($2,356/wk)
- **Work schedule:** T/W/Th/F/Sa, 9AM, 10-hour shifts
- **Makeover categories (3+):** Outfits (everyday/formal/swimsuit/sleepwear/athletic), Hairstyles (with highlights/colors), Makeup (with opacity slider), Male facial hair
- **Work cycle:** Discuss Fashion social -> Enter Create a Sim toolkit -> Modify client appearance -> Client evaluates + tips
- **Lv3 unlock:** Makeover jobs outside salon (map view)
- **Lv6 unlock:** Give Fashion Advice social (temporary new looks)
- **Lv10 reward:** Always Stylin' permanent moodlet
- **Evil Stylist:** Give Bad Makeover interaction (dreadful looks, client furious)
- **Career rewards:** Stylish Trophy (10 Sims), High Style Ribbon (50 Sims), Fashionable Medal (100 Sims), Dazzling Looking Glass (25 Sims at Lv12)

### 6. Medical Career (Hybrid Active/Rabbit-hole, Lv1-10)
- **Career progression:** Organ Donor ($128/day) -> World Renowned Surgeon ($2,400/day)
- **Hybrid mechanics:** Standard work tones at hospital + active community activities
- **Medical Tones (7):** Business As Usual, Work Hard, Watch TV in Ready Room, Chat with Co-Workers, Do Boss's Paperwork, Sleep in Ready Room, Meet Medical Personnel
- **Active community tasks (Lv3+):**
  - Free Clinics: set up at community lots, Diagnose interaction with portable x-ray
  - Vaccinations: Vaccinate interaction at community lots
  - Medical Outreach: befriend and advise locals
  - Local Emergencies: respond to choking Sims with Medical Attention interaction
- **Socials:** Give Medical Advice (helps pregnant Sims), Brag About Being a Doctor
- **Advancement metrics:** Mood + Logic Skill + Medical Journals Read (from Lv4+)
- **Career rewards:** Medical Trophy of Honor (3 Inoculation Jobs), Medical Trophy of Valor (10 Building Emergencies), Medal of Mending (Diagnose 40 Sims), Skeletal Endowment (6 Free Clinics at Lv10)

### 7. Inventing Skill (Lv1-10)
- **Practiced at:** Scraptronic Workbench (scrap bin attached)
- **Scrap sources:** Junkyard, detonated objects, direct purchase
- **Crafting progression:** Simple widgets -> Complex devices -> Special inventions
- **Key inventions:** Widgets, Toys, Harvester, Time Machine (Lv10)
- **Detonation mechanic:** Sims can detonate household objects for scrap; risk of explosion/fire
- **Blueprint system:** Discover blueprints through experimentation; manufacture multiples

### 8. Sculpting Skill (Lv1-10)
- **Practiced at:** Pedestrian Sculpting Station ($1,100)
- **Material progression:** Clay (Lv1) -> Stone -> Metal -> Ice (highest level)
- **Sculpture types:** Abstract, Classical, Modern, Topiary (living plant sculptures)
- **Quality ratings** affect sale value and environmental score
- **Unfinished projects** can be scrapped or resumed
- **Large objects** (ice blocks, topiary bushes) placed directly on station

### 9. Skill-Based Careers (Self-Employment)
- **Register at City Hall** for any skill-based career
- **8 career tracks with Lv1-10 advancement:**
  - Writer: Fan Fiction Drafter ($75) -> Blockbuster Writer ($50,000 advancement threshold)
  - Painter: Paint Splasher ($60) -> Master of the Brush ($60,000)
  - Gardener: Cucumber Smasher ($30) -> Agricultural Mastermind ($80,000)
  - Fisher: Rod and Reel Rookie ($30) -> Legendary Piscator ($80,000)
  - Inventer: Electrocution Victim ($30) -> Fourth Dimensional Architect ($60,000)
  - Sculptor: Questionable Potter ($60) -> Master Sculptologist ($60,000)
  - Photographer: Papa/Mama Paparazzi ($20) -> Snap Dragon ($60,000) (World Adventures required)
  - Nectar Maker: Grape Kicker ($30) -> Master Nectar Craftsman ($35,000) (World Adventures required)
- **Advancement:** Based on total Simoleons earned from selling skill products
- **Career rewards:** $25K threshold per skill earns unique trophy

### 11. Retiring & Pensions
- Sims can retire from any career at any level
- **Pension:** Daily income smaller than active wages, varies by career level
- Enables pursuing skills and hobbies full-time while maintaining income

### 12. Additional Systems
- **Bake Sale:** Child Sim career; place bake sale table, Stock and Tend interactions, set prices for vanilla/chocolate goods, adults Purchase
- **Laundry system:** Dirty clothes -> Washing Machine -> Dryer or Clothesline; Clean Clothes moodlet
- **Tattooing:** Ink-in-Comfort 3000; Offer/Get/Remove Tattoo interactions; Lv6 Handiness upgrade (Inkinization)
- **Part-Time Work:** Bookstore Clerk, Graveyard Clerk, Grocery Clerk, Spa Receptionist, Spa Specialist (3 levels each, 4 hours/day, 4 days/week)
- **School system:** Tones (Normal Effort, Work Hard, Slack Off, Meet New Friends, Talk to Friends, Sleep in Class), Homework system, Grades (C start, A for 3 days = Honor Roll moodlet), Detention for cheating/sleeping, Age transition trait selection based on grades

## Systems & Architecture

### Active Profession Work Cycle
```
Receive Job/Alert
  -> Travel to Location (drive/walk)
    -> Assess Situation (scan/interview/measure)
      -> Perform Core Task (capture ghost/fight fire/renovate/makeover)
        -> Client/System Evaluation (scoring)
          -> Payment + Career XP
            -> Return to Station/Home
              -> Skill Development (off-hours practice)
```

### Skill-Based Economy
```
Skill Practice (home/community lot)
  -> Create Product (painting/novel/sculpture/invention/fish/produce/photo/nectar)
    -> Sell Product (direct sell, consignment store, gallery)
      -> Income accumulates toward Career Level thresholds
        -> Threshold reached = Level Up + Bonus payment
          -> Higher levels = higher-value products = faster advancement
```

### Twinbrook World Structure
```
Twinbrook (bayou-themed world):
  -> Residential areas (swamp-side homes, trailer parks, estates)
  -> Commercial district (City Hall, Salon, Fire Station, Police Station)
  -> Industrial zone (Junkyard for scrap, Laundromat)
  -> Community School / Hospital / Science Facility
  -> Parks and fishing spots
  -> Cemetery (part-time work location)
```

## Core Insight

Ambitions reveals that the fundamental problem with the original Sims 3 career system was not complexity but *control*. By making work playable rather than simulatable, the expansion transforms careers from a progress bar into the primary gameplay loop. The Architectural Designer profession is the most architecturally interesting: it temporarily hands the player a modified version of the game's own Build/Buy Mode tools, then scores them on how well they use those tools to satisfy an NPC's trait-derived preferences -- essentially turning the game's creative sandbox into a scored challenge mode. The Investigator profession imports detective game mechanics (surveillance, evidence gathering, interrogation) into a life simulator, demonstrating the engine's genre flexibility. The Skill-Based Career system is a quiet revolution: it formalizes what players were already doing (selling paintings, fish, novels) into a parallel economy with its own progression track, validating creative play as a legitimate career path within the simulation. The Traditional Careers section reveals the Tones system as a strategic layer that transforms passive wait-for-promotion into active choice-making during work hours. The expansion's deepest insight is that work and play are not opposed categories in game design -- they are the same system viewed from different angles, and the most engaging careers are those where the player cannot tell where one ends and the other begins.
