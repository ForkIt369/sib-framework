# Deep Dive: The Sims 2 Bon Voyage - Prima Official Game Guide

> **Source:** The Sims 2 Bon Voyage Expansion Pack - Prima Official Game Guide
> **Publisher:** Prima Games
> **Platform:** PC
> **Type:** Strategy Guide / Systems Reference

---

## Core Essence

Bon Voyage extends The Sims 2 beyond the home lot and local neighborhood into a full **travel and tourism simulation** with three culturally distinct vacation destinations, each with unique activities, collectables, secret lots, and NPCs. The expansion introduces a vacation economy -- airfare pricing fluctuates by destination, season, and party size; accommodation types range from free campgrounds to premium hotels with NPC staff; and every vacation is scored on a meter that determines lasting post-trip benefits. At its deepest layer, Bon Voyage is about **extraction and accumulation**: Sims travel to harvest mementos, gestures, dances, massages, and souvenirs that confer permanent passive bonuses back home. The vacation is the mechanism; the ShowMeOff Rack of Glory is the payoff. Bigfoot is the hidden treasure -- a 10-skill demigod who can be recruited into a household through sheer friendship grinding, introducing a permanently overpowered entity who breaks the game's normal progression curves.

## The Vibe

Bon Voyage reads like a theme park operations manual crossed with an anthropological field guide. Each destination is a curated cultural theme park -- Twikkii Island is tropical romance and beachcombing, Takemizu Village is martial discipline and spiritual contemplation, Three Lakes is rugged outdoor survivalism. The guide treats vacations as resource extraction expeditions: every destination has a specific loot table of gestures, dances, foods, and souvenirs to systematically harvest. The Rack of Glory is the endgame -- a trophy wall that converts travel experiences into permanent stat bonuses. There is an undercurrent of colonial tourism: Sims arrive, extract cultural artifacts and special abilities, and return home enriched. The Bigfoot system is the guide's crown jewel, described with a mixture of awe and exploitation -- here is a creature with perfect stats in everything, who can be befriended and relocated to serve as a permanent household asset. The hotel system introduces service-industry logistics into a life simulator. The vacation scoring mechanic transforms leisure into a performance metric. Even relaxation is optimized: the Zen Garden meditation literally freezes all Need decay, turning spiritual practice into a min-max exploit. The Pirate Ship is a $32,767 money sink that doubles as a social interaction hub and souvenir generator. The whole expansion treats travel as a scoring system where fun is measured, culture is collected, and every sunset has a return on investment.

## New Mechanics Introduced

### 1. Vacation Booking & Travel System
- Booked via phone; select destination, duration, and travel companions
- **Airfare pricing formula:** Base cost modified by destination, season (peak vs. off-peak), party size, and travel duration
- Vacation clock starts on arrival and counts down; Sims return home when time expires or manually
- Three accommodation types: Hotels (paid, NPC staff, room service), Campgrounds (free, tents/campfires), Vacation Homes (purchased real estate, permanent ownership)
- Vacation destinations are sub-neighborhoods attached to the base neighborhood

### 2. Vacation Scoring & Benefit Tiers
- Real-time vacation quality meter tracking positive and negative experiences
- **3 benefit tiers:** Carefree (minor mood boost), Refreshed (moderate mood + relationship boost), Productive (mood + relationship + skill gain boost post-vacation)
- Score increases from: fulfilling Wants, successful social interactions, collecting mementos, learning skills/activities
- Score decreases from: Fear triggers, Need failures, negative social outcomes

### 3. Three Destination Ecosystems
- **Twikkii Island (Island):** Beach activities, Hula dance, seashell combing, pirate ship, tropical foods, romance-boosting souvenir bonuses
- **Takemizu Village (Far East):** Bow gesture, Tai Chi, Zen Garden meditation, Spiritual Shrine, Ninja riddles, skill-boosting souvenir bonuses
- **Three Lakes (Mountain):** Chest Thump gesture, Slap Dance, campfire activities, log cabin living, Bigfoot encounter, social-boosting souvenir bonuses
- Each destination has unique foods, NPCs, collectables, and secret lots

### 4. Memento & ShowMeOff Rack System
- 45 vacation mementos earned through specific activities across all destinations
- Displayed on the ShowMeOff Rack of Glory ($850 object)
- **Memento Monikers** progression: Bumbling Tourist (0) -> Amateur Adventurer -> Curious Explorer -> Intrepid Globetrotter -> Savvy Traveler (42)
- **Passive bonuses by collection type:**
  - 5 Far East souvenirs = faster skill gain
  - 5 Mountain souvenirs = better non-romantic social outcomes
  - 5 Island souvenirs = better romantic social outcomes
  - 5 shells = slower Bladder Need decay
  - 5 Mr. Mickles dolls = periodic bee hallucinations (penalty)

### 5. Collectable Activities & Skills
- **Gestures:** Bow (Far East), Hang Loose (Island), Chest Thump (Mountain) -- cultural greetings learned from destination NPCs
- **Dances:** Hula (Island), Tai Chi (Far East), Slap Dance (Mountain) -- improve hidden Dancing skill
- **Massages:** Hot Stones, Acupressure, Deep Tissue -- $500 each to learn from Masseuse NPCs
- **Beachcombing:** 12 shell types ranging up to $430 value; jewelry finds possible
- **Treasure Digging:** Buried chests worth $5,000; maps leading to secret lots
- **Pirate Ship Plunder:** Captain's Log ($675), Gold Doubloon ($550), Treasure Map ($2,500) from Plunder Captain's Cabin

### 6. Zen Garden & Meditation
- $1,250 object; Comfort 2 / Fun 1 / Environment 10
- **Groom** interaction for maintenance
- **Meditate** interaction: freezes all Need decay and Mood changes while active
- Experienced meditators visually levitate during meditation
- Functions as a powerful Need-management exploit

### 7. Spiritual Shrine of Odd Blessings
- $2,500 interactive object
- **Toss Coin mechanic:** 75% base accuracy + 5% per Body skill point; Body 5+ guarantees a hit
- Outcome distribution: 50% nothing, 10% each for 5 outcomes
- **Possible outcomes:** Great Secret (hidden benefit), Vast Fortune ($1,000 cash), Lucky Career (promotion boost), Bees (swarm attack), Scratching (persistent itch)
- Shrine changes color to indicate outcome type

### 8. Pirate Ship System
- $32,767 purchase price (most expensive object in the expansion)
- **Interactions:** Explore, Walk the Plank, Man the Wheel, Raise/Lower Jolly Roger, Fence with Cutlass, Climb Crow's Nest, Plunder Captain's Cabin
- Plunder Captain's Cabin yields souvenirs or spawns the Ghost Captain NPC
- Walk the Plank functions as a social humiliation mechanic

### 9. Voodoo Doll (Mr. Mickles)
- Souvenir acquired from the Witch Doctor on secret lots
- **8 curse interactions** targeting a selected Sim
- Each curse has a backfire chance that redirects the effect to the user
- 5 Mr. Mickles displayed on the Rack of Glory trigger bee hallucinations (a penalty, not a bonus)

### 10. Beach Interactions
- **Build Sandcastle:** Quality 0-10 based on Creativity skill; visible output scales with quality
- **Comb for Seashells:** 12 shell types with varying Simoleon values
- **Get In Ocean:** Builds Body skill; carries drowning risk
- **Sunbathe:** 40% chance suntan (lasts 48 hours), 30% chance sunburn (lasts 24 hours, -Comfort), 30% no effect

### 11. Secret Lots & NPCs
- Hidden lots within each destination, accessed by finding treasure maps or meeting specific conditions
- **Ninja** (Far East): Teaches Teleport ability through riddle challenge
- **Wise Old Man** (Mountain): Provides Dragon Legend Scroll
- **Witch Doctor** (Island): Rewards Mr. Mickles Voodoo Doll
- **Unsavory Charlatan:** Pickpockets $150; Logic skill enables detection; Memory system grants immunity to recognized Charlatans; Report interaction bans them for 3 days
- **Tour Guide:** Provides destination information and directions

### 12. Bigfoot
- Found on secret lots at Three Lakes
- **Recruitment requirements:** Daily Relationship 55+ and Lifetime Relationship 5+ to request move-in
- Ages normally but cannot reproduce
- Ignores Hygiene Need entirely (never decays)
- **10 in all skills and all badges** -- permanently maxed
- Always receives promotion at work regardless of performance
- Generates generosity gifts for household members
- **Species interaction modifiers:** Dogs love Bigfoot (Daily +20, Lifetime +20, Mood +15), Cats hate Bigfoot (Daily -30, Lifetime -20, Mood -15), Children benefit (Daily +12, Lifetime +5, Mood +4)
- **Can** become zombie or vampire; **Cannot** become werewolf or PlantSim
- Cannot get sunburned, cannot be abducted by aliens, cannot use mirrors or Change Appearance
- Destructive behavior triggered when Hunger or Fun Needs reach desperation levels
- Unique socials: Bigfoot Hug (Fun + Social + relationship boost, depletes Hygiene), auto-Scare (wakes up to scare Thieves)

### 13. Object-Based NPCs
- **Bellhop:** Spawns at hotel front desk; handles check-in/check-out and room service orders
- **Food Stand Chefs:** Destination-themed food vendors; restricted to behind-counter area
- **Ghost Captain:** Appears from Pirate Ship's Plunder Captain's Cabin; requires Daily 7 to befriend; teaches Sea Chantey; limited social interactions; persists in Relationship panel across vacations; cannot be called, invited home, or married
- **Fire Dancer:** Spawns from Scorchin' Matt object; performs fire dance show or teaches the fire dance to Sims
- **Housekeeper:** Hotel cleaning NPC; arrives morning, works all day, leaves at night; cleans and repairs common areas and vacant rooms; fully social -- can be befriended, married, and moved in
- **Masseuse/Masseur:** One per massage table; knows Swedish massage plus local specialty (Hot Stones/Acupressure/Deep Tissue depending on destination); only Swedish available on non-vacation community lots; fully social -- can be befriended, married, and moved in

### 14. Hotel System
- Hotels require: Front Desk (spawns Bellhop), guest rooms with Solidity Doors, beds, and bathroom facilities
- Check-in/check-out managed through Bellhop interaction
- Room service available for food delivery
- Housekeeping NPC maintains common areas and vacant rooms
- Per-night charges based on room quality and hotel tier

### 15. Build Mode: Beaches & Roofs
- **Beach lot templates:** 3x5, 4x5, 5x5, 5x6 sizes; must have road on one side and water on the other; at least half on land; template must overlap water by at least one tile
- **5 beach ground covers:** Ocean Depths, Ocean Shallows, Ocean's Edge, Sandier Grass, Standard Issue Sand (wet and dry editions)
- Cannot build structures on beach portions of lots
- **Pagoda Roofs:** Square or rectangular footprint only; cannot intersect with other roof types
- **Roof Decorations:** Aspiring Spire, Dragon on the Roof, Fun for the Roof I-IV; destination-themed textures (Corrugated Steel = Mountain, Thatched = Island, Asian Tile = Far East)

## Systems & Architecture

### Vacation Economy Pipeline
```
Phone_Booking(destination, duration, party_size, season)
  -> Airfare = base_cost * destination_mod * season_mod * party_mod * duration_mod
  -> Travel_To_Destination
  -> Accommodation_Selection:
       Hotel (per_night_charge, NPC_services)
       | Campground (free, minimal_amenities)
       | Vacation_Home (owned, no_recurring_cost)
  -> Vacation_Clock_Start(duration)
  -> Activity_Loop:
       Score += want_fulfillment + memento_collection + social_success
       Score -= fear_triggers + need_failures
  -> Vacation_End -> Score_Maps_To_Tier:
       Carefree | Refreshed | Productive
  -> Apply_Post_Vacation_Benefits(tier)
```

### Memento Accumulation & Passive Bonus System
```
Activity_Completion -> Award_Memento(type, destination)
  -> Place_On_Rack_Of_Glory
  -> IF count(Far_East_souvenirs) >= 5: skill_gain_multiplier++
  -> IF count(Mountain_souvenirs) >= 5: non_romantic_social_bonus++
  -> IF count(Island_souvenirs) >= 5: romantic_social_bonus++
  -> IF count(shells) >= 5: bladder_decay_rate--
  -> IF count(mr_mickles) >= 5: bee_hallucination_chance++

Memento_Moniker = f(total_mementos):
  0-9:   Bumbling Tourist
  10-19: Amateur Adventurer
  20-29: Curious Explorer
  30-41: Intrepid Globetrotter
  42-45: Savvy Traveler
```

### Spiritual Shrine Probability Engine
```
Toss_Coin:
  accuracy = min(100%, 75% + (Body_skill * 5%))
  IF Body >= 5: accuracy = 100%
  IF miss: no_effect
  IF hit:
    roll = random(0, 100):
      0-49:   Nothing
      50-59:  Great_Secret (hidden_benefit)
      60-69:  Vast_Fortune (+$1,000)
      70-79:  Lucky_Career (promotion_boost)
      80-89:  Bees (swarm_attack)
      90-99:  Scratching (persistent_itch)
    shrine_color = outcome_indicator
```

### Bigfoot Interaction Matrix
```
Bigfoot_State:
  Skills: ALL = 10
  Badges: ALL = 10
  Hygiene: IGNORED (no decay)
  Employment: AUTO_PROMOTE (always promoted)

  Pet_Modifiers:
    Dogs:  Daily +20, Lifetime +20, Mood +15
    Cats:  Daily -30, Lifetime -20, Mood -15
    Children: Daily +12, Lifetime +5, Mood +4

  Occult_Compatibility:
    Zombie:    YES
    Vampire:   YES
    Werewolf:  NO
    PlantSim:  NO
    Alien_Abduction: NO
    Sunburn:   NO

  Desperation_Behavior:
    IF Hunger < threshold OR Fun < threshold:
      -> destructive_actions()

    Daily_Relationship >= 55
    Lifetime_Relationship >= 5
```

## Core Insight

Bon Voyage reveals that **travel in simulation is resource extraction dressed as leisure**. Every destination is a loot table with culturally themed drops -- gestures, dances, souvenirs, foods, massages -- and the Rack of Glory is the inventory system that converts collected experiences into permanent mechanical advantages. The vacation scoring system transforms rest into a performance metric: even relaxation has a grade, and that grade determines post-trip stat bonuses. The three destinations are not meaningfully different in structure -- they are reskinned extraction zones with parallel loot tables, each optimized for a different stat category (romance, skills, social). The Zen Garden breaks the Need management system entirely by freezing all decay during meditation, turning a spiritual practice into a min-max exploit. The Spiritual Shrine is a slot machine with Body skill as the house edge reducer. Bigfoot is the expansion's most radical addition: a permanently maxed entity who trivializes the game's core progression loops of skill-building and career advancement. The fact that Bigfoot requires high relationship scores to recruit but then delivers infinite value reveals the expansion's deepest assumption -- that the most powerful reward for social investment is a creature who makes all other systems unnecessary. The hotel and NPC staffing systems preview the service-industry simulation that Apartment Life would later expand with landlords and butlers. The whole expansion treats vacation as an optimization problem: minimize cost, maximize loot, return home with passive bonuses that compound across all future gameplay.
