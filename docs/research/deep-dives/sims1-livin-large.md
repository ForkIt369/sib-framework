# Deep Dive: The Sims Livin' Large - Prima's Official Strategy Guide

> **Source:** *The Sims Livin' Large: Prima's Official Strategy Guide*
> **Publisher:** Prima Games (2000)
> **Platform:** PC
> **Coverage:** The Sims (base game) + Livin' Large Expansion Pack
> **Pages:** 202 (includes full base game content + expansion-specific chapters)
> **Foreword:** Will Wright (creator of The Sims)

---

## Core Essence

The Livin' Large guide is a **comprehensive reference** covering both the original Sims and the Livin' Large expansion in a single volume. It re-documents the entire base game while integrating expansion-specific content -- 5 new career tracks, new objects (including supernatural/science-fiction themed items), new NPCs, new neighborhoods, and new gameplay mechanics around potions, the Crystal Ball, the Chemistry Lab, the Servo robot, and the Tragic Clown. The expansion pushes The Sims from pure domestic realism toward **whimsical chaos** -- aliens abduct Sims through telescopes, chemistry experiments produce transformative potions, a Crystal Ball summons a fortune-telling genie, and a malevolent Tragic Clown haunts unhappy households. The guide frames Livin' Large as the game's evolution from life management into emergent storytelling through engineered randomness.

## The Vibe

Essentially the same voice as the Original Sims guide (written by/affiliated with the same Prima Games editorial team), but with an expanded sense of possibility. The Livin' Large sections convey a spirit of experimentation and risk-taking that the base game's careful motive management discourages. Where the original guide is about optimization, the Livin' Large additions introduce **controlled chaos** -- objects that have random or semi-random outcomes, NPCs that show up uninvited, and mechanics that can dramatically alter a household's trajectory in a single event. Will Wright's foreword establishes the philosophical foundation: The Sims as a "mirror" reflecting players' own lives back at them, but Livin' Large is the funhouse mirror that distorts the reflection into comedy and catastrophe.

### Chemistry Lab Potion System
The Home Chemistry Lab ($19 for the kit) builds Logic skill while producing random potions. Potion outcomes are **weighted by Logic skill level** -- higher Logic = better chance of beneficial results.

**Potion Colors and Effects:**
| Color | Effect | Risk Level |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Blue** | Motive Enhancer -- instantly fills a random motive | Beneficial |
| **Yellow** | Personality Inversion -- flips all 5 traits to their opposites | Chaotic |
| **Green** | Cure -- heals Guinea Pig Disease | Beneficial (situational) |
| **Red** | Love Potion -- attracts nearest Sim romantically | Mixed |
| **Pink** | Invisibility -- Sim becomes temporarily invisible | Neutral/Fun |
| **White** | Motive Subtractor -- drains a random motive | Harmful |
| **Chartreuse** | Unknown/random minor effect | Variable |
| **Brown** | Monster potion -- unknown chaotic effect | Harmful |
| **Orange** | Evil Clone -- creates hostile duplicate of the Sim | Dangerous |

The Chemistry Lab is one of the most cost-effective skill-building objects in the game -- $19 for unlimited Logic skill training plus potential free motive restoration.

### Crystal Ball & Genie System
Madame Blahbatfry's Crystal Ball ($760) summons a genie who offers spell-based interactions:

| Spell | Effect | Risk |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Friend Spell** | Instantly adds +50 to Relationship Score with a specific Sim | Low |
| **Money Spell** | Grants random amount of Simoleons | Low-Medium |
| **Love Spell** | Target Sim falls in love with caster | Medium |
| **Earth Spell** | Plants garden gnomes around the lot | Low |
| **Air Spell** | Random effect on Air/weather | Medium |
| **Fire Spell** | Can start a fire on the lot | HIGH |
| **Water Spell** | Fills pools or creates water effects | Medium |
| **Leisure Spell** | Boosts Fun motive | Low |
| **Work Spell** | Affects career performance | Medium |

**Riddle System:** The genie also poses riddles based on personality traits:
- **Neat riddles** (levels 150-156 in index)
- **Outgoing riddles** (levels 155-156)
- **Active riddles** (level 155)
- **Playful riddles** (level 154)
- **Nice riddles** (levels 154-155)

### Servo Robot
The Servo robot ($15,000 equivalent, or acquired through gameplay) is an autonomous household assistant:
- **Cleaning:** Washes dishes, mops floors, takes out trash
- **Gardening:** Waters plants, maintains lawn
- **Repair:** Fixes broken objects
- **Cooking:** Prepares "Robo-Food" (functional but no personality-based cooking quality)
- **Malfunction Risk:** Random breakdowns requiring Mechanical skill to repair
- Servo does not have motives, personality, or social needs
- Servo operates 24/7 without rest
- Can be directed to specific tasks or left on autonomous mode

### Tragic Clown Mechanic
- **Trigger:** Household Mood persistently low + Tragic Clown painting present on lot
- **Behavior:** Wanders property, cries, makes messes, depresses all Sims in proximity
- **Duration:** Stays indefinitely until removed
- **Removal Methods:**
  1. Sell or delete the Tragic Clown painting (primary method)
  2. Improve household Mood above threshold
  3. Move the painting to a neighbor's lot (transfers the Clown)
- The Clown is essentially a **negative feedback loop amplifier** -- he appears when Sims are unhappy and makes them unhappier, creating a death spiral if not addressed

### Ghost Mechanics (Enhanced)
- Same base mechanics as original (11 PM spawn, 1-in-8 chance per urn/tombstone)
- Ghosts can now **howl** in addition to scaring
- Ghost behavior is more varied: may interact with objects, create messes
- **Removal:** Sell urn/tombstone (ghost gone forever); move urn/tombstone indoors (indoor ghost) or outdoors (outdoor ghost = tombstone)
- Ghosts of Sims who died different ways may exhibit different behaviors

### Alien Abduction
- **Trigger:** Using Horrorwitz "Star-Track" Telescope at night
- **Effect:** Sim vanishes temporarily, returns with altered personality traits or mood
- **Frequency:** Random chance each night use session
- **Prevention:** Don't use telescope after dark (daytime use is safe and builds Logic normally)

### Guinea Pig Disease
- **Source:** Neglected guinea pig cage (dirty, unfed)
- **Symptoms:** Sim turns green, sneezes, refuses activities
- **Progression:** Can be fatal if untreated for extended period
- **Cure:** Another household member must use Medicine Cabinet; also curable via green Chemistry Lab potion
- **Prevention:** Keep guinea pig cage clean and pet fed

### Family Life Cycle (Complete)
**Single Sim Strategy:**
- Military career recommended (highest starting pay, Body-focused = single skill path)
- Bachelor Pad essentials: Bed, Toilet, Shower, Fridge, Stove, Counter, Bookshelf, Phone, Trash Can, Alarm Clock, Smoke Detector, Burglar Alarm
- Minimize house size to reduce bills
- Focus first 10 days on pure skill building before socializing

**Leaving Single Life:**
- Relationship 70+ required for romance options
- Sufficient positive romantic interactions (Kiss, Hug, Flirt) to trigger Propose option
- Proposal success depends on Relationship Score and Mood

**Marriage:**
- Wedding ceremony occurs on lot
- Spouse moves in with their existing job and bank account (financial windfall)
- Household income doubles immediately
- Shared household means more hands for chores and child-rearing

**Conception:**
- Keep smooching until "Try for Baby" or similar option appears
- Babies are "Fun" objects initially
- Baby requires feeding and interaction when crying
- Neglect triggers Social Worker who permanently removes baby

**Raising Children:**
- Kids attend school 8am-3pm automatically
- Report Card tracks grades (A+ to F)
- Grades drop one letter per bad-mood school day
- F grade = Military School ($1,000 deducted)
- Kids can build skills via Bookshelf, Chess, Creative objects
- Kids cannot use: Bar, Coffee/Espresso, Exercise Machine, Hot Tub (Outgoing 7+ rule doesn't apply), Medicine Cabinet
- Kids can use: Dollhouse, Toy Box, Play Structure, Train Set, most Fun objects

### The Object Depreciation System (Complete A-to-Z)
The guide contains the definitive depreciation table for every purchasable object in the game:
- **Purchase Price:** What you pay
- **Initial Depreciation:** Immediate value loss on placement
- **Daily Depreciation:** Value lost per Sim-day
- **Depreciation Limit:** Minimum resale value floor

This system means all objects are **wasting assets** except artwork (paintings and sculptures can appreciate or depreciate randomly). The 24-hour buyer's remorse refund window is the only way to avoid Initial Depreciation loss.

### Visitor Activities System
The guide provides a complete table of which activities visiting Sims will autonomously join:
- **Joinable Activities:** Hot Tub, Pool, Chess, Train Set, Basketball, TV watching, Stereo dancing, Piano listening, Pool Table, Pinball, Dollhouse, Play Structure
- **Personality-Driven Preferences:** Active visitors prefer physical activities; Lazy prefer sedentary; Outgoing seek social objects; Serious prefer skill objects
- **Exit Conditions:** Visitors leave when their Mood drops below a threshold or when all available activities are exhausted
- **Drop-in Visitors:** Random chance of visitors appearing at lot edge (sidewalk) around 89% of the time during certain hours

### Sim Economics (Chapter 7)
**Fire, Theft, and Accidents:**
- **Cooking Fire:** Chance based on Cooking skill; Toaster Oven more fire-prone than Stove; Microwave never starts fires
- **Fireplace Fire:** Anything combustible on adjacent tile catches fire (not immediately, but eventually)
- **Electrocution:** Based on Mechanical skill (0 skill = 100% chance on TV repair; 3+ skill = 1% chance)
- **Burglar:** Random nighttime event; Burglar Alarm detects motion within 5 exterior tiles
- **Flood:** Broken plumbing objects; Neat Sims fix faster
- **Bills:** Every 3 days based on lot value; 10-day non-payment = Repo Man

**Net Worth Calculation:**
- Total household value = Cash + Sum of all object current values (after depreciation)
- Bills calculated as percentage of total lot value

## Core Insight

Livin' Large reveals The Sims' design philosophy at its most explicit: the game is a **probability playground** where the player manages not certainty but *variance*. The expansion systematically introduces objects and mechanics that trade predictability for potential -- the Chemistry Lab can restore a motive for free or create an evil clone; the Crystal Ball can grant instant friendship or burn the house down; the Telescope can build Logic or trigger alien abduction. Every new Livin' Large mechanic follows the same pattern: **higher risk, higher potential reward, lower player control**. This reframes the base game's careful optimization as just one valid strategy among many. The Tragic Clown is the purest expression of this philosophy -- a punishment mechanism disguised as a gag, a negative feedback loop that punishes the player for allowing sustained unhappiness. The expansion's message is clear: in The Sims as in life, playing it safe produces stable mediocrity, while taking risks produces either spectacular success or entertaining disaster. The game doesn't care which -- both are valid content.
